Our Process

From idea to keys in hand.

Step by Step

How an Elbow Room project unfolds.

Every project follows the same backbone: clarify, design, permit, build, support. Within that, we tailor details to your property, city, and goals.

01
2–3 weeks

Discover & Define

We start by getting clear on your property, budget, and how you want to use your ADU.

  • Site review (photos, survey, utilities)
  • Use case + budget alignment
  • High-level feasibility in your city
  • Model / collection recommendations
02
4–8 weeks

Design & Approvals

We translate your ideas into drawings, selections, and a clear scope of work.

  • Base model + options selected
  • Preliminary layout & elevations
  • Finish selections from Design Center
  • Budget & scope sign-off
03
6–12+ weeks

Permits & Sitework

We handle the paperwork and prep the site so construction can move efficiently.

  • Full permit set & engineering
  • Submittals to your city / county
  • Utility coordination & tap plans
  • Grading, forms, foundation, inspections
04
3–5 months

Build & Turnover

We build your ADU, manage trades, and keep you informed until keys are in your hand.

  • Framing, rough-ins, insulation, drywall
  • Exterior cladding, windows, roofing
  • Interior finishes, fixtures, appliances
  • Final inspections, punch list, handoff

During construction

  • ✓Weekly progress updates
  • ✓Inspection schedule & results
  • ✓Change order approvals in writing
  • ✓Final walkthrough & punch list

Your role

We guide you, but we’ll still need you for key decisions and approvals.

  • Share goals, budget, and non-negotiables
  • Provide any existing surveys or documents
  • Make design & finish decisions on schedule
  • Approve change orders and milestones
  • Clear access to the build area

Our role

We handle the heavy lifting—from drawings and permits to trades and inspections.

  • Feasibility and code/ setback checks
  • Drawings, engineering, and permit submittals
  • Scheduling and managing all trades
  • Quality control and site supervision
  • City inspections and final approvals
