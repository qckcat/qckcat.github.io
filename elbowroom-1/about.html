About Elbow Room

Backyards built with intention.

Elbow Room is a Central Texas builder focused on one thing: turning underused yards into Studios, Suites, and Residence ADUs that actually work for the way you live, earn, and host.

Based in: Central Texas · San Antonio · Austin · Surrounding cities

See the work See the process

What we focus on.

Instead of trying to build everything, we specialize in three kinds of backyard structures:

  • • Studio – Accessory Structure, no plumbing (office, studio, gym)
  • • Suite – Accessory Structure with bath (guest, in-law, pool suite)
  • • Residence – Full ADU (kitchen + bath, rental or family use)

That narrow focus lets us stay sharp on ADU rules, permitting paths, and what actually works on real Central Texas lots.

Our Story

From “we need more space” to a clear path.

Elbow Room was built for homeowners who were tired of vague bids, unclear timelines, and builders who treated backyards as an afterthought instead of a primary living space.

We’ve sat at the same kitchen tables you have—trying to figure out where aging parents will stay, how to support kids coming back home for a season, or how to make a property carry a little more of its own weight without turning it into a full-time job.

Instead of “one-off” backyard construction, we decided to specialize. By focusing on accessory structures and ADUs, we’ve been able to standardize the parts of the process that should feel predictable—models, finish levels, permitting paths—while still leaving room for the kind of design details that make a space feel like it belongs to your house, your street, and your family.

The result is a builder that thinks like a designer, communicates like a project manager, and executes like a seasoned construction team. You get one partner to walk you through models, Design Center choices, city approvals, and the full build, all the way to punch list and warranty.

What Guides Us

The way we build matters as much as what we build.

We work in your yard, near your family, and inside your city’s rules. That demands a different kind of builder.

01 · Clarity

Real numbers, real expectations.

We’d rather be honest about ranges and constraints than win a project with a lowball guess. Our Design Center and model structure keep every decision tied to a budget and a timeline.

02 · Respect

We build where you live.

Construction shouldn’t feel like chaos. That means clear communication, predictable milestones, and treating your property and neighbors like our own throughout the build.

03 · Design

Backyards deserve good design.

Studios, Suites, and Residences are small but they play a big role in your daily life. We sweat light, privacy, storage, and the overall feel of the space—not just square footage.

04 · Focus

We stay in our lane.

By specializing in Studios, Suites, and Residence ADUs, we can keep refining one system instead of stretching ourselves thin on unrelated projects.

05 · Partnership

We’re in this together.

We see ourselves as partners, not vendors. We’ll give you the same guidance we’d give a friend or family member building on their own property.

06 · Longevity

Thinking beyond move-in day.

Every Studio, Suite, and Residence is designed with a “what if” lens—what if your needs change, what if family moves in, what if you decide to rent. We plan for those pivots early.

What We Build

One builder, three structure types.

We call them Studio, Suite, and Residence to make the options feel human, not like a codebook. Under the hood, they’re three different ways to use your lot and navigate your city’s rules.

Studio · Accessory Structure · No plumbing Suite · Accessory Structure · With bath Residence · ADU · Kitchen + bath

Studio

Accessory Structure · No plumbing

Offices, creative rooms, therapy spaces, gyms—quiet, focused space just outside your back door, without plumbing complexity.

Suite

Accessory Structure · With bath

Guest and in-law suites, pool houses, teen hangouts—spaces that give people privacy while staying closely tied to the main house.

Residence

ADU · Full residence

Self-contained homes with a kitchen, bath, and private entry—built for long-term family use, rental income, or both over time.
