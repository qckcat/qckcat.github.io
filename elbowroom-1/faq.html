Start with the basics

Common questions, honest answers.

These FAQs are meant to help you decide if a Studio, Suite, or Residence is worth exploring, not pressure you into a build. Use them as a gut-check before you reach out.

What we build
What’s the difference between a Studio, Suite, and Residence?
Studio is an accessory structure without plumbing—think office, creative room, gym, or therapy space. Suite adds a bathroom, making it ideal for guests, in-laws, pool-side use, or teens. Residence is a full ADU with a kitchen and bath, functioning as a small, independent home for family or rental use.
Do I have to know which one I want before reaching out?
No. Many people come to us thinking “maybe a backyard office” and end up aligning on a Suite or Residence after we talk through how they really plan to use the space over time. Part of our job is helping you choose the right lane for your lot and goals.
Where do you currently build?
We focus on San Antonio, Austin, and select Central Texas cities where we understand the permitting paths and ADU rules. If you’re slightly outside those areas, reach out anyway—we’ll give you a clear yes/no and, if needed, suggestions for next steps.
How long does a project typically take?
Timelines vary by city and scope, but very broadly:

• Design + permitting: often measured in months, not weeks.
• Construction: typically a few months once we’re in the ground.

One of the first things we’ll do is outline a realistic timeline for your specific city, structure type, and season.
Cost, process & design
How much does a Studio, Suite, or Residence usually cost?
Costs depend on size, site conditions, and finish level, but in general:

• Studios (no plumbing) sit at the lighter end of the spectrum.
• Suites add bathroom plumbing and finish complexity.
• Residences (full ADUs) involve kitchen, bath, and utility planning similar to a small home.

We’ll talk about budget ranges early and keep every decision tied back to a realistic overall number for your project—not just the base structure.
What’s your process from first conversation to move-in?
We keep it simple and transparent:

1) Discover: Learn your goals, lot conditions, and rough budget.
2) Align: Map your needs to Studio, Suite, or Residence models.
3) Design & permits: Refine the layout, submit to your city, and handle permitting.
4) Build: Manage construction, inspections, and quality control.
5) Close-out & warranty: Final walkthrough, punch list, and post-move support.

You can see the full outline on our Process page.
Will my Studio, Suite, or Residence actually match my house?
That’s one of the main reasons people choose us. We treat your Studio, Suite, or Residence as an extension of your home, not a random shed. That means caring about proportions, rooflines, window placement, siding, and the overall feel from the house and yard— not just the interior finishes.
Do you help with interiors and furnishings?
Yes, we can help you think through interior layouts, lighting, storage, and key finish decisions. Depending on scope, we can also support furnishing and decor planning so the space is truly move-in ready for your guests, family, or tenants.

If you’re torn between Studio, Suite, and Residence, it’s very normal. Most clients refine their choice after we’ve talked about family, work, hosting, and long-term goals—not just square footage.

Studio · Suite · Residence

Which one sounds most like you?

You don’t have to memorize terminology. Use this as a quick gut check and bring your answers into our first conversation.

Studio

Accessory Structure · No plumbing
  • • You need quiet, focused work or creative space.
  • • You’re tired of using the dining table as an office.
  • • You want a gym, studio, or practice room that isn’t in the garage.
  • • Plumbing complexity isn’t necessary for how you’ll use the space.

Suite

Accessory Structure · With bath
  • • You host family or guests regularly.
  • • You’re thinking about longer stays for parents or in-laws.
  • • You want a pool house or teen hangout with real privacy.
  • • A bathroom on-site would make the space truly usable.

Residence

ADU · Full residence
  • • You want a self-contained home with kitchen + bath.
  • • You’re thinking about rental income or a long-term family plan.
  • • You’re comfortable talking about a larger investment for flexibility.
  • • You see this as part of your property’s long-term story.
