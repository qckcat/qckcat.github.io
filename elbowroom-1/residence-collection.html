Residence Collection.

Full accessory dwelling units with kitchens, living rooms, and 1–2 bedrooms. Backyard homes that live like a small house, built for long-term tenants or family.

Structure type: ADUs (Full Residences) · Typical range: $150k–$197k

A real home, not a “tiny house experiment.”

These plans are designed to feel like a complete home: dedicated bedrooms, full kitchens, real storage, and layouts that make sense for daily life—not just Pinterest.

Clients use Residence models for long-term rentals, multi-generational living, downsizing on their own property, or a “backup plan” for future needs.

Built to match the main home in quality.

We bring the same finish level and construction standards we’d want in a main house: solid surfaces, thoughtful lighting, and details that age well.

We align exterior architecture and color with your existing home or create a complementary contrast—either way, it feels intentional.

Big Picture

An ADU as part of your financial plan.

We don’t treat your ADU as just a cool project. It’s a serious asset. We’ll talk through rent ranges, different use scenarios, and how this fits into your larger plan for the property.

Scenario Use What Matters Most
Long-term tenant 12+ month leases Privacy, durability, low maintenance finishes
Family first Parents, adult children Comfort, accessibility, proximity and sightlines
Flex mix Sometimes family, sometimes rental Flexible layout and storage, design that ages well

Running numbers with you.

We’ll help you think through realistic rent ranges based on your city and the model you choose. No wild promises—just grounded, experience-based ranges.

From there, we’ll look at rough payback periods and help you decide whether a Suite or a full Residence is the better fit.
