Step by Step

From first call to move-in ready.

We run a fully turn-key process: design, permitting, and construction under one roof. You always know which step you’re in, who’s doing what, and what needs to happen next.

Step 1 · Discovery & Fit

Walk the yard · Pick your lane.

Studio vs Suite vs Residence · Site realities · High-level budget and goals.

We start with a conversation and a look at your property. We’ll talk about who you’re building for (you, family, or tenants), your timeline, and your ideal monthly payment or return. From there, we’ll narrow in on Studio, Suite, or Residence as your primary path.

Choose a model, then bend it to your life.

Named floor plans · Yard fit · Entry / privacy · Parking concepts.

We’ll match you with a starting floor plan from our Studio, Suite, or Residence collections (for example: The Mark, The Retreat, or The Compound) and test it on your lot. We’ll look at access, privacy, views, and how the new structure relates to your main home.

Model selection Site fit & orientation Parking & access concepts
Step 3 · Design Center & Pricing

Dial in finishes and get a real range.

Design Center session · Finish package selection · Budget clarity.

Inside our Design Center, we’ll walk through curated finish packages and key upgrades. This is where we align aesthetics with numbers—so you see how each decision shapes your overall range before you sign anything.

Design Center session Finish packages Value-engineering options
Step 4 · Approvals & Permitting

Turn ideas into stamped plans.

City coordination · Engineering · Utility plans · ADU rules.

We handle the unglamorous part: coordinating with your city, engineers, and utilities. For ADUs, that means navigating specific rules around setbacks, height, parking, and utilities. For Austin and San Antonio, we already know the common paths—and the common landmines.

Step 5 · Build & Walkthroughs

Watch it go vertical.

Groundwork · Foundation · Framing · Finishes · Quality checks.

Once permits are in hand, our build team takes over. We’ll communicate at each milestone— ground, foundation, framing, rough-ins, and finishes—and schedule walkthroughs so you can see the space taking shape in real time.

Where you are vs. where we plug in.

Some clients come in with a raw idea, others with spreadsheets and mood boards. The process above is flexible enough to meet you where you are—and structured enough to keep things moving.

  • • Just starting: We’ll spend more time in Steps 1–3.
  • • Already dreaming in detail: We’ll fast-track into design and permitting.
  • • Investor mindset: We’ll frame decisions in terms of rent, ROI, and exit.

Structure Types

One process, three kinds of buildings.

We don’t reinvent the wheel for every project. Instead, we run the same proven process across three structure types, tuning the scope, timeline, and approvals to fit the category that matches your goals.

Studio – Accessory Structure · No plumbing Suite – Accessory Structure · With bath Residence – Full ADU · Kitchen + bath

Studio

Accessory Structure · No plumbing

Focused, flexible space without plumbing complexity. Perfect for work, creative time, or a quiet room that still lives close to home.

  • • Fastest permitting path in many cities
  • • Ideal for offices, therapy, studios, or gyms
  • • Strong daily-life upgrade without tenant logistics

Suite

Accessory Structure · With plumbing

A self-contained room with a bathroom. Great for aging parents, returning kids, or guests who need more privacy than a couch or spare room can offer.

  • • Bathroom and sometimes a kitchenette
  • • Comfort and autonomy for guests or family
  • • Can double as a high-end guest or pool suite

Residence

ADU · Full residence

A complete home with its own kitchen, bath, and entry. Designed to behave like a second small house on your lot for family, tenants, or future flexibility.

  • • Maximum long-term flexibility and value
  • • Best for rentals and multi-generational families
  • • More detailed permitting and utility coordination
Timeline & Expectations

What the calendar usually looks like.

Every city and lot is different—but after dozens of projects, we’ve found a general rhythm that holds up across most Studio, Suite, and Residence builds.

Weeks 0–4

Discovery, model, and Design Center.

We confirm fit, choose a structure type, pick a model, and walk through finishes and pricing so you understand the full picture before committing to construction.

Weeks 4–12+

Plans, engineering, utilities, and permitting.

We develop the full plan set, coordinate engineering, handle utility planning, and work with your city on permits—answering questions and addressing corrections along the way.

Months 3–9+

Construction, walkthroughs, and move-in.

Once everything is approved, we move through site work, foundation, framing, rough-ins, finishes, and final inspections—keeping you updated and involved at each milestone.

Timelines for a Studio can be shorter; a Residence ADU with more involved utilities and city rules will lean longer. The important part is that you always know which step you’re in and what has to happen next.
