Interior design overview

A clear interior roadmap, not a random showroom visit.

Instead of handing you a catalog and saying “good luck,” we guide you through a simple interior structure: start with the big surfaces (floors, paint), layer in kitchens or kitchenettes, then refine with appliances, fixtures, lighting, and hardware.

Each interior decision stays anchored to:

  • • The structure type (Studio, Suite, or Residence).
  • • How the space will actually be used (daily living, guests, work, rental, etc.).
  • • A base package level that matches your budget band.

From there, we can selectively upgrade where it matters to you most—without losing the cohesion that makes the space feel calm, finished, and intentional.

Base packages

Three interior package levels to start from.

Behind the scenes, we align every Studio, Suite, and Residence with an interior package: a sensible starting point for how “dressed up” your interior will be. From there we can selectively dial things up or keep them clean and simple.

Package 01

Essential

A clean, durable base that feels calm and intentional—never like a bare minimum rental.

  • • Core flooring, paint, and cabinet selections.
  • • Thoughtful but simple hardware and fixtures.
  • • Ideal for Studios and straightforward Suites.
Package 02

Elevated

A step up in texture, contrast, and detail—without tipping into “show house” preciousness.

  • • Expanded palette of flooring and tile.
  • • Upgraded cabinet options and hardware.
  • • Great fit for Suites and many Residences.
Package 03

Signature

The most curated version of our interiors: more nuance in materials, layering, and detail.

  • • Signature palette and special material options.
  • • Coordinated upgrades across multiple categories.
  • • Ideal for flagship Residences or long-term family use.

Each interior subpage will reference these package levels, so you can see exactly what “Essential,” “Elevated,” and “Signature” mean for kitchens, kitchenettes, baths, flooring, paint, and fixtures.

Aligned with how you’ll live

Interiors that match Studios, Suites, and Residences.

The interior package you choose will look slightly different depending on whether you’re building a Studio, Suite, or Residence. This isn’t just about square footage—it’s about how the space needs to perform.

Studios

Work-forward or hobby-forward spaces that still feel warm and finished.

  • • Flooring and paint do most of the visual heavy lifting.
  • • Kitchenette is optional and scaled to use.
  • • Fixtures kept streamlined and easy to maintain.

Suites

Guest-forward or flex living spaces where baths and kitchenettes matter more.

  • • Bathrooms and kitchenettes take center stage.
  • • Furniture layout and lighting get extra attention.
  • • Great candidates for Elevated interior packages.

Residences

True small homes where interiors need to support daily living for years.

  • • Full kitchens and baths with more storage detail.
  • • Furniture circulation and long-term wear considered.
  • • Often Elevated or Signature packages.
