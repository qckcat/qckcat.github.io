Central Texas focus

We build where we can understand the rules—and then confirm them.

Elbow Room is a Central Texas builder first. That means we’re tuned into the mix of city codes, ETJs, HOAs, and “that one inspector” realities that shape what you can actually put in your backyard.

Within Central Texas, we treat every new address as a small research project. San Antonio and Austin give us strong anchors; from there, we extend out into neighboring cities and suburbs where the code and process still support our level of work.

In practice, that looks like:

  • • San Antonio – Our deepest bench of experience for Studios, Suites, and Residences.
  • • Austin – ADU-heavy environment with specific zoning and utility realities.
  • • Central Texas cities – A wide radius of communities where we’re willing to do the homework.

No matter the city, what we’re designing is the same:

  • • Studios – Accessory Structures without plumbing.
  • • Suites – Accessory Structures with bathrooms.
  • • Residences – Full ADUs with kitchen + bath.

Live in Central Texas? It’s almost always worth asking.

If you’re anywhere in the Central Texas orbit, don’t assume you’re “too far” until we’ve had a chance to look. When you reach out, share:

  • • Your property address
  • • Studio / Suite / Residence (or “not sure yet”)
  • • Any quirks you already know about the lot

We’ll respond with a clear yes, no, or “yes, with a bit more research.”

Every address gets its own research

“We build everywhere in Central Texas” doesn’t mean “we guess.”

We’re comfortable saying we build across Central Texas because we do the work to understand each new address. That might mean digging into a smaller city’s code, talking with utilities, or mapping out how your lot sits inside an ETJ or HOA.

Some places will feel familiar right away. Others will require an extra round of questions before we finalize a path for your Studio, Suite, or Residence.

Areas that often move quickly

  • • San Antonio and surrounding suburbs.
  • • Austin and nearby communities.
  • • Central Texas cities with clear ADU or accessory structure pathways.

Areas that trigger extra homework

  • • Properties near shifting ETJ boundaries.
  • • Lots with complex easements, slopes, or floodplain overlays.
  • • HOA-heavy neighborhoods with strict architectural guidelines.

Either way, we’ll walk you through what we find instead of hand-waving it. If something in your jurisdiction makes a Studio, Suite, or Residence a poor fit, we’ll say that clearly and early.

What happens after you send your address.

When you reach out from anywhere in Central Texas, we’ll:

  • • Confirm that your property sits inside our Central Texas radius.
  • • Flag obvious code or access constraints we see right away.
  • • Outline a realistic path for your Studio, Suite, or Residence—or explain why not.

The goal isn’t to make the map look bigger on a slide. It’s to make sure each project we take on is one we can stand behind for years.
