Financing & Budget

Ways to fund your Studio, Suite, or Residence.

We’re not a lender—and we like it that way. Our role is to help you see the full investment clearly, then ask smarter questions of whoever you trust with your money.

Our philosophy on money and building.

We build Studios, Suites, and Residences that feel like they belong on your property for decades. That means being honest about cost, timelines, and what your yard and city will actually support.

  • • No teaser pricing that ignores real site conditions.
  • • No pressure to “stretch” into something that doesn’t feel right.
  • • Clear alignment between your financing choice and your long-term plan.
First things first

Start with the whole picture, not just a monthly payment.

Before you talk to a lender, it helps to be clear on what you’re building (Studio, Suite, or Residence), where you’re building (San Antonio, Austin, or nearby), and what kind of ballpark you’re comfortable with.

Once we understand your goals and site conditions, we’ll help you frame a realistic total investment range. From there, you and your lender can decide how best to structure the money.

  • • Studios usually sit at the lighter end of our project spectrum.
  • • Suites add bathroom complexity and tend to land in the middle band.
  • • Residences (full ADUs) behave more like a small custom home in your yard.

No matter which path you use to fund it, our goal is the same: a Studio, Suite, or Residence that feels worth what it cost every time you walk into the backyard.

We are not a lender.

We don’t sell in-house financing or steer you toward a specific bank. Instead, we:

  • • Help clarify a realistic project range for your yard and city.
  • • Coordinate with your lender when they need construction details.
  • • Keep you in the driver’s seat with financial decisions.

Good next step:

Bring us your address, a rough comfort range (even just “low / mid / high”), and whether Studio, Suite, or Residence feels closest.

From there, we can help you talk with your lender in concrete terms instead of guesses.

Common funding paths

Three ways people typically pay for their project.

Every household is different. These lanes are not recommendations or financial advice—just a clear way to name the options many Studio, Suite, and Residence clients talk about with their lenders.

Cash or savings

Simple · Fastest to move

Paying directly from cash or savings can keep things straightforward, especially for lighter Studio builds or smaller Suites.

Good fit when:
  • • You’ve already earmarked funds for home or yard upgrades.
  • • You prefer not to add another monthly obligation.
  • • You want maximum flexibility and speed on decisions.
Things to consider:
  • • How this project fits into your other near-term priorities.
  • • Whether you want to reserve cash for future phases or furnishings.
  • • If a hybrid approach (cash + another product) feels more balanced.

Home equity

HELOC · Home equity loan

Many clients talk with their bank about using current equity to invest in a Studio, Suite, or Residence that upgrades both daily life and long-term property value.

Good fit when:
  • • You have available equity in your home.
  • • You like the idea of tying this project to your primary property asset.
  • • You want flexibility in how quickly you draw funds during construction.
Questions to ask your lender:
  • • “How would a Studio, Suite, or Residence affect my borrowing capacity?”
  • • “What would my payment look like across different project sizes?”
  • • “Are there draw schedules or inspection requirements I should know about?”

Renovation / construction products

Project-specific financing

Some lenders offer renovation or construction-focused products that can be a fit for larger projects, especially full Residences (ADUs).

Good fit when:
  • • You’re building a Residence ADU with kitchen + bath.
  • • Your lender offers products designed for major improvements.
  • • You plan to hold the property long term or use it for rental income.
Questions to ask your lender:
  • • “Do you have renovation or construction loan options for ADUs?”
  • • “What does your draw schedule look like, and how do you work with builders?”
  • • “How are interest, fees, and timelines structured for this type of project?”
Budget & structure types

How Studio, Suite, and Residence relate to investment.

We’ll talk actual numbers once we know your address and site conditions, but it can help to think in terms of “where your project likely lives” on the spectrum. These aren’t quotes—just a way to orient.

Studio

Accessory Structure · No plumbing
  • • Typically at the lighter end of our project range.
  • • Best for focused work, creative space, or a gym.
  • • Often a good fit for cash, savings, or smaller equity draws.
  • • Reduced complexity since there’s no bathroom or kitchen.

Suite

Accessory Structure · With bath
  • • Sits in the middle band of our project spectrum.
  • • Adds plumbing, fixtures, and finishes for a real guest experience.
  • • Commonly funded through a mix of equity and savings.
  • • Great for multi-use: guests, in-laws, pool house, or teens.

Residence

ADU · Full small home
  • • Behaves more like a small custom home investment.
  • • Includes kitchen, bath, utilities, and full mechanical planning.
  • • Often aligned with equity-based or construction-focused products.
  • • Best for long-term flexibility and potential rental income.

Once we’ve walked your goals and lot conditions, we’ll frame a specific range for your Studio, Suite, or Residence. From there, you and your lender can decide whether cash, home equity, a project-specific product—or a combination—makes the most sense.

Our role in your financial picture

We handle the build. You choose how to fund it.

We take our responsibility seriously: every dollar you put into a Studio, Suite, or Residence should feel respected—from the first permitting conversation to the last punch-list item.

That means being clear about what we do and don’t do around financing, and making sure the people who advise you financially have what they need from us.

  • • We do help you understand the full scope of work and realistic investment range.
  • • We do coordinate documentation and communication with your lender as needed.
  • • We do keep your project plan grounded in what your yard and city will support.
  • • We don’t provide financial or tax advice.
  • • We don’t steer you into a specific lender or product.
  • • We don’t pressure you into a project size that doesn’t feel aligned.

Helpful questions to bring to a lender.

Once we’ve talked, you might ask:

  • • “If we invest in a Studio / Suite / Residence, how does that interact with our current mortgage?”
  • • “Which product would you use for a backyard structure at this scale?”
  • • “What would our payment look like at different project sizes?”

When your lender needs specifics—from project scope to draw schedules—we’re happy to be in that conversation with you.
