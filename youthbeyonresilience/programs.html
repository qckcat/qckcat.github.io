Our Programs

Five Pillars of Transformation for Haiti's Youth

Each CAMPS pillar offers a different doorway into healing and hope. Together, they create a full ecosystem that keeps youth away from gangs and anchored in community.

Trauma-Informed, Youth-Led, Community-Rooted

Holistic support Evidence-informed Local staff & partners Safe spaces in gang-controlled areas

CAMPS is more than a set of activities—it’s a framework that reaches the whole person. Every rehearsal, match, counseling circle, and community project is designed to help youth build emotional resilience, leadership, and alternatives to gang recruitment.

  • Consistent rhythm: weekly programming youth can rely on.
  • Trusted adults: Haitian staff, counselors, and mentors.
  • Skill-building: from conflict resolution to creative expression.
Age focus: 10–18 years old Model: group-based, trauma-informed, family-aware

Where We Work

Anchored in High-Risk Communities, Rooted in Hope

Our three program centers are located in neighborhoods heavily impacted by gang activity. Each one is a safe, creative, and stabilizing presence for youth and families.

Croix-des-Bouquets

Foundation Ste. Cécile

High-risk area ~280 youth
Programs: Mental health, Music for Inclusion, Football for Peace, arts intensives, community service.
Delmas 33

Communal School of Delmas

Gang-controlled zones nearby ~260 youth
Programs: Counseling, civic engagement, personal development, leadership training & cultural events.
Pétion-Ville

Holy Trinity Music School

Music hub ~260 youth
Programs: Orchestra & choir, drum ensembles, arts intensives, performance-based community outreach.

Port-au-Prince: 85% Under Gang Influence

In a city where most neighborhoods live with the daily pressure of gang control, these three centers function as circles of safety—places where youth can breathe, learn, and imagine a different future.

800+ youth actively enrolled 3 strategic hubs in the city Families & schools engaged as partners

A Day in the Life

What Safe, Structured Support Feels Like

CAMPS programming gives Haitian youth a steady rhythm: predictable, safe spaces where they can build friendships, practice skills, and be deeply known by caring adults.

2:30 PM
Arrive & Exhale
Youth arrive from school or home and check in with staff. A simple question— “How are you arriving today?”—opens space for honest sharing.
3:00 PM
Care Circle or Counseling Session
Small group circles or 1:1 counseling address stress, grief, and fear. Youth learn grounding tools and language for what they’re feeling.
4:00 PM
Arts, Music, or Sports Track
One youth tunes a violin; another laces up cleats. Whether on the field or in rehearsal, every activity is anchored in relationship and skill-building.
5:30 PM
Reflection & Commitments
The day closes with gratitude, reflection questions, and practical commitments youth can carry back home—to siblings, neighbors, and friends.

“When the shooting starts near our house, I remember what we practiced here—how to breathe, how to talk to my little brother so he doesn’t get as scared. This place gave me tools I didn’t know I needed.”

— 15-year-old participant, Mental Health & Music for Inclusion

Every day, youth are choosing a third path.
Not gangs. Not hiding in fear. But healing, creativity, community, and leadership. Your support makes this daily rhythm possible.
