PROGRAM OVERVIEW

The Five Pillars of CAMPS

Each pillar offers a different “doorway” into transformation. Together, they form a safe, structured ecosystem where Haitian youth can heal, grow, and lead.

C

Civic Engagement & Community Service

Youth explore leadership, community service, and advocacy, learning how to shape a more just and peaceful Haiti.

Learn more
A

Arts & Cultural Expression

Music, visual arts, and Haitian cultural traditions become tools for healing, identity, and joy.

Learn more
M

Mental Health & Wellness

Trauma-informed counseling and psychosocial support help youth and families process grief, fear, and stress.

Learn more
P

Personal Development & Empowerment

Life skills, financial literacy, and mentorship equip youth to make strong choices about their futures.

Learn more
S

Sports & Recreation

Football for Peace and other sports teach teamwork, conflict resolution, and healthy outlets for energy.

Civic Engagement & Community Service

Leadership Development Community Projects Economic Literacy
  • Workshops on leadership, citizenship, and non-violent conflict resolution.
  • Community service projects co-designed with youth and local partners.
  • Partnerships with organizations like Groupe Croissance and local institutions.

Target outcomes: Youth see themselves as solution-makers, understand their rights, and gain tools to influence their neighborhoods without turning to violence.

Arts & Cultural Expression

Music for Inclusion Visual Arts Haitian Drum & Dance
  • Orchestra and choir rehearsals through Music for Inclusion.
  • “Drawing the Hope” visual arts workshops and exhibitions.
  • Haitian drum circles and cultural performances for community events.

Target outcomes: Youth experience beauty, belonging, and pride in Haitian culture, while building discipline, teamwork, and emotional resilience.

Mental Health & Wellness

Group Counseling Individual Sessions Family Support
  • Group counseling three times per week for youth in high-risk areas.
  • Individual sessions with trained psychologists and social workers.
  • Tools and workshops for caregivers navigating trauma and instability.

Target outcomes: Youth gain coping strategies, emotional literacy, and a sense of safety that allows them to focus on learning, creativity, and relationships.

Sustain Mental Health & Wellness

Personal Development & Empowerment

Life Skills Workbooks Mentorship
  • Life skills workshops on decision-making, boundaries, and healthy relationships.
  • Personal development workbooks tailored for Haitian adolescents.
  • Mentorship and coaching focused on education, vocation, and long-term goals.

Target outcomes: Youth develop a sense of agency, practical skills, and vision for who they want to become — and how to get there.

Invest in Personal Development

Sports & Recreation

Football for Peace Team Building Conflict Resolution
  • Football for Peace leagues that mix youth from rival neighborhoods.
  • Sports-based workshops on teamwork, respect, and emotional regulation.
  • Tournaments and events that showcase positive role models and community pride.

Target outcomes: Youth channel energy into healthy competition, build cross-neighborhood friendships, and practice nonviolent problem-solving.

WHERE WE WORK

Three Centers in the Heart of Port-au-Prince

YBR programs are based in communities where gang recruitment is most aggressive — offering safe, predictable spaces where youth can gather, learn, and grow.

Foundation Ste. Cecile • Croix-des-Bouquets
Serving youth living on the edge of gang-controlled territories, with daily arts, counseling, and sports.
Youth reached: 250+ per year • Programs: CAMPS, family support, community events
Communal School of Delmas • Delmas 33
A hub for civic engagement and mental health programming in a densely populated, high-stress neighborhood.
Youth reached: 300+ per year • Programs: After-school CAMPS, leadership labs
Holy Trinity Music School • Pétion-Ville
Home of Music for Inclusion, where orchestras and choirs bring together youth from across the city.
Youth reached: 250+ per year • Programs: Arts & culture, performances, mentorship
A DAY IN THE LIFE

What Hope Looks Like on a Tuesday

A typical day in YBR programs weaves together safety, routine, creativity, and community — giving youth a structured alternative to the chaos outside.

2:00 PM
Arrival & Check-In

Youth arrive from school, share a snack, and check in emotionally with staff using simple, trauma-informed tools.

3:00 PM
CAMPS Sessions

Some youth head to music rehearsal, others to counseling circles, civic workshops, or football practice.

4:30 PM
Reflection & Skill-Building

Groups gather to reflect on what they learned, practice conflict resolution, and set personal goals.

5:30 PM
Heading Home with Hope

Youth leave in small groups, supported by peers and staff, carrying new tools, friendships, and possibilities.
